TriggerEngage vs Brevo

The short version

If you want a hosted, affordable email + SMS tool with a free tier and transactional sending built in, Brevo is a solid all-rounder. If you’re a developer-led team that wants event-based journeys, self-hosting, and no per-send or per-contact bill, TriggerEngage is the more focused, ownable fit.

What Brevo does well

Brevo packs a lot into an affordable, hosted package: email campaigns, SMS, marketing automation, transactional sending, and a light CRM, with a free tier to start. For a small business that wants email and SMS in one place without running infrastructure, it’s a practical, budget-friendly choice.

Where TriggerEngage is different

Brevo is a hosted SaaS metered by sends and contacts; TriggerEngage is open source and self-hosted with no per-send fee. Both do email and SMS, but TriggerEngage is event-first — journeys react to your app’s events — and it runs on your infrastructure so your data and sending reputation are yours.

Where TriggerEngage wins

  • Self-hosted, open source — own your data & reputation
  • No per-send or per-contact bill
  • Event/behavior-first journeys
  • Laravel-native: install via Composer
  • Push as a first-class channel, plus email + SMS

Where Brevo wins

  • Hosted — nothing to run; quick to start
  • Generous free tier for small senders
  • Transactional + marketing email in one place
  • Built-in light CRM and broad templates

When Brevo is the better choice

Choose Brevo when you want a hosted, low-cost way to send email and SMS without managing infrastructure, when its free tier fits your volume, or when a light built-in CRM is handy. TriggerEngage is the better pick when you want event-based journeys, self-hosting, and to avoid a bill that scales with your sends.

Pricing & cost of ownership

Brevo is priced mainly by emails sent, with a free daily tier and paid plans above it (check their site for current limits). TriggerEngage has no per-send license fee — you self-host and pay your own infrastructure and sending provider. At higher volumes, self-hosting the engine and buying raw sending capacity is usually cheaper.

Frequently asked questions

Is TriggerEngage a Brevo alternative?
Yes for event-based email and SMS — the difference is that TriggerEngage is open source and self-hosted rather than a hosted SaaS, and it’s event-first. If you want a hosted all-in-one with a free tier, Brevo is the easier start.
Does TriggerEngage send transactional email like Brevo?
TriggerEngage focuses on behavioral, journey-based messaging; you connect your own sending provider, which can also handle transactional mail. Brevo bundles transactional and marketing sending in one hosted service.
Is there a free tier?
TriggerEngage is free and open source outright — there’s no tier because you self-host it. Brevo’s free tier is a hosted plan with daily send limits.
Which is cheaper at scale?
Usually TriggerEngage, since there’s no per-send license fee — you pay infrastructure plus raw sending. Brevo’s simplicity is the trade-off for its metered pricing.
