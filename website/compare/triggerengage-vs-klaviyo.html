The short version

If you run an e-commerce store (especially Shopify) and want deep revenue attribution and prebuilt store flows, Klaviyo is superb. If you’re building any kind of product and want to trigger messaging from your own events — without a per-profile bill and while owning your data — TriggerEngage is the more flexible, developer-first choice.

What Klaviyo does well

Klaviyo is built for online stores. Its Shopify integration, predictive analytics, revenue reporting, and library of e-commerce flows (browse abandonment, post-purchase, win-back) are best-in-class, and its combined email + SMS make it a natural fit for direct-to-consumer brands.

Where TriggerEngage is different

Klaviyo is optimized around the e-commerce data model — stores, orders, products. TriggerEngage is data-model agnostic: you define your own events and profiles, so it fits a SaaS app, a marketplace, a fintech, or a health platform just as well as a store. It’s open source and self-hosted with no per-profile fee, and it builds behavioral segments from whatever events your product emits. If you’re not primarily an online store, that generality matters.

Where Klaviyo wins

  • Deep Shopify & e-commerce integrations
  • Predictive analytics and revenue attribution
  • Prebuilt store flows out of the box
  • Managed sending and strong e-commerce reporting

When Klaviyo is the better choice

Choose Klaviyo when you run an online store and want e-commerce messaging that works on day one — Shopify sync, product feeds, revenue-per-recipient reporting, and store-specific flows. That depth is hard to replicate, and if selling products is your core, Klaviyo’s specialization is a real advantage. TriggerEngage is the better pick when your product isn’t a store, or when self-hosting and cost control outweigh prebuilt e-commerce features.

Pricing & cost of ownership

Klaviyo is priced by active profiles, with email and SMS metered as you scale (see their site for current pricing) — excellent value when messaging drives clear store revenue, but it climbs with your list. TriggerEngage carries no per-profile fee because you host it yourself; you pay for infrastructure and your sending provider. For non-store products, or lists where only some contacts are actively messaged, that’s often the cheaper path.

Frequently asked questions

Is TriggerEngage a good Klaviyo alternative?
It’s a strong alternative if you want event-based messaging for a product that isn’t primarily an online store, or if you want to self-host and avoid per-profile pricing. If you’re a Shopify store leaning on Klaviyo’s e-commerce features, Klaviyo is likely the better fit.
Does TriggerEngage integrate with Shopify?
Not natively today — TriggerEngage is data-model agnostic and works from the events your app sends, so a store would forward its events (orders, carts) rather than use a prebuilt Shopify sync. Klaviyo’s native Shopify integration is more turnkey for stores.
Can TriggerEngage do email and SMS in one flow?
Yes — email, SMS, and push are all first-class channels, so a single journey can span them based on what the user does. That’s the same multi-channel model Klaviyo uses for email + SMS.
Do I own my customer data with TriggerEngage?
Yes — because you self-host, all profiles and event history stay on your own infrastructure rather than in a vendor’s cloud.
