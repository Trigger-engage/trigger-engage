The short version

If you’re a large organization that needs omnichannel messaging at massive scale with dedicated support and can fund an enterprise contract, Braze is a category leader. If you’re a startup or mid-size team that wants the same event-based journeys, segments, and A/B testing without sales-led pricing — and would rather own the stack — TriggerEngage gets you there open-source and self-hosted.

What Braze does well

Braze is built for scale and breadth: real-time, high-volume messaging across email, push, in-app, SMS, and more, with sophisticated orchestration, experimentation, and analytics. Large consumer brands rely on it, and its enterprise support and reliability are serious strengths.

Where TriggerEngage is different

Braze is enterprise software with enterprise pricing and procurement. TriggerEngage delivers the same fundamental model — events in, journeys out, with lifecycle sequences, segments, and A/B splits — as an open-source package you self-host. There’s no sales call, no seat count, and no per-profile meter; you install it and run it. It won’t match Braze’s scale-out infrastructure or its breadth of enterprise features, and that’s the honest trade.

When Braze is the better choice

Choose Braze when you’re operating at large enterprise scale, need guaranteed SLAs and dedicated support, or require the depth of omnichannel orchestration and compliance tooling that a major platform provides. For big consumer apps sending at high volume, that infrastructure and support are worth the price. TriggerEngage is aimed at the many teams that want event-based engagement long before — or without ever — needing an enterprise contract.

Pricing & cost of ownership

Braze uses custom, sales-led enterprise pricing, typically a significant annual commitment sized to your volume and channels (contact them for a quote). TriggerEngage has no license cost at all — you self-host, paying only for infrastructure and your sending providers. The gap is largest exactly where it hurts startups most: early, when an enterprise contract is out of reach but you still need real lifecycle messaging.

Frequently asked questions

Can TriggerEngage replace Braze?
For many startups and mid-size teams, yes — it delivers event-based journeys, multi-channel sends, segmentation, and A/B testing without an enterprise contract. At very high volume with strict SLA and support needs, Braze’s enterprise infrastructure is hard to match.
How does TriggerEngage scale?
You control scaling because you host it — it runs on Laravel’s queue and scheduler and scales horizontally with your infrastructure. That’s more hands-on than Braze’s managed platform but far cheaper, and it’s plenty for most non-enterprise workloads.
Is there dedicated support like Braze offers?
No — TriggerEngage is open source with community support and documentation, not a vendor with enterprise SLAs. If dedicated support is a hard requirement, that favors Braze.
Why choose an open-source option over Braze?
Cost and ownership: no sales-led contract, no per-profile bill, and your data stays on your infrastructure. For teams that don’t need enterprise scale, that’s a compelling trade.
