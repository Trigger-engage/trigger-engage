# Meet the founder

Hi, I'm Raphael — founder and software engineer. If you follow my work, you know me as the mind
behind Mytherapist.ng, one of Africa's biggest health-tech startups, and OpinionPadi, Africa's
foremost user-led marketing-research platform.

Beyond those two startups, there's a lot of other work I've built and shipped for other startups
through my agency, MixedFraction.

## The problem

One problem has stood out consistently across my years of building and growing these products:
finding a simple platform that helps me onboard, nurture, and communicate with users through
their journey with my products.

Traditional (bi-weekly) newsletter email marketing is dead. Well, not exactly dead — it's just
not very effective, at least not as effective as event-based messaging.

## What I needed

I knew how annoying this problem was, and I needed a solution, so I went looking.

My requirements were simple — or so I thought. I needed a platform that lets me:

1. Trigger email message sequences based on user actions or events.
2. Send push notifications based on events.
3. Send SMS messages based on events.
4. Tweak and customize the copy separately for each channel (SMS, push, email).
5. Embed the SDK directly into my own backend/project, so I can fire events, register users,
   and so on from my own codebase.

### Existing solutions

If you ignore the outrageous cost of most email marketing platforms (Mailchimp, AWeber, and the
like), you'll find that they let you define email onboarding sequences but lack push
notifications. So at best you settle for email only (a few offer SMS as an option) — you'd hardly
find any with push notification support.

And then I found something really well thought out: Customer.io.

Customer.io had every single thing I needed — push notifications, SMS, even down to PHP/Laravel
SDKs. It was unbelievable, yet very effective.

### The problem with Customer.io

It was expensive. The smallest plan is $100 a month, and that only covers up to 12,000
profiles/users — which, realistically (at least by third-world / Nigerian standards), isn't very
affordable or sustainable.

### Where Customer.io shone

It'd be unfair not to mention that Customer.io has a very generous startup program that lets you
use their starter plan free of charge for a year, with 30,000 profiles included.

I benefited from those startup credits and had access to Customer.io for the year Mytherapist.ng
was in the program. Unfortunately, we couldn't sustain it once that access expired.

## Why Trigger Engage

As expiry drew nearer, I started to daydream. What if...

What if there were an alternative to Customer.io that wasn't so expensive? What if it were
open-source? What if I could self-host it — or even install it inside my Laravel repository and
have it bundled with my codebase?

That's why Trigger Engage is a reality today.
